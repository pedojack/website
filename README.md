# The Jack Gerritsen (ex- KG6IRO) Pedo-Files

## pedojack.com & chomojack.com

This collection of public information about the convicted sex offender and ham radio nuisance Jack Gerritsen is maintained by <tom@pedojack.com>
***

Jack Gerritsen, *ex-KG6IRO*, born in 1936 and residing in Bell, California, has a documented history of criminal convictions spanning sex offenses and repeated radio interference. According to California's Megan's Law sex offender registry, he is a lifetime-registered sex offender with a prior-code conviction under California Penal Code § 647a for annoying or molesting a child under 18 years of age. Registry entries also reference indecent exposure under the current statute Penal Code § 314.1 (lewd or indecent exposure). Gerritsen has publicly claimed he was wrongfully convicted, asserting government lies and innocence, and at times linking the case to his anti-Vietnam War protests. Details of the exact conviction date and full court records from that era are limited in digitized public sources, but the offense appears tied to events potentially around or before 1970.

In addition to the sex offense, Gerritsen faced significant state-level consequences for radio-related crimes. In December 1999, he was arrested by the California Highway Patrol for interfering with police radio transmissions. He was convicted in June 2000 in Los Angeles County Municipal Court (case involving California Penal Code violations for obstructing or interfering with police communications) and sentenced to 38 months in prison. He was released around July 2003 but violated probation terms related to possessing radio equipment, leading to further short-term custody.

Gerritsen's most extensive legal troubles stemmed from persistent unlicensed radio operation and malicious jamming after his state prison release. The FCC issued multiple forfeiture orders totaling over $50,000 in proposed and upheld fines, including a $10,000 penalty (affirmed in 2004) for operating without authorization in the Amateur Radio Service, and two separate $21,000 fines for willfully interfering with authorized radio communications. In May 2005, he was arrested by the FBI (with FCC assistance) on federal charges. A jury convicted him in December 2005 on six counts: one felony for malicious interference with a U.S. government communications system (involving Coast Guard frequencies) and five misdemeanors involving willful interference and transmitting without a license (affecting MARS, Red Cross, amateur repeaters, and other public safety channels). In September 2006, U.S. District Judge R. Gary Klausner sentenced the then-70-year-old Gerritsen to 7 years in federal prison plus $15,225 in fines. He represented himself at trial and later appealed aspects of the case.

These crimes—ranging from the earlier sex offense registration to repeated deliberate disruption of emergency, military, and amateur radio communications—resulted in substantial prison time, monetary penalties, equipment confiscation, and permanent loss of any amateur radio privileges. Gerritsen often broadcast prerecorded political messages, harassment, and profanity, which courts ruled went far beyond protected speech.
